Levels of Organisation

Revision Notes

Organisms (such as plants and animals) are made up of a variety of things which work together to perform important functions. The pieces of the organism puzzle are as follows:

  • DNA contains instructions and information for cells to function. All cells contain DNA in some capacity.
  • Cells are the building blocks of living organisms. They perform the functions that an organism needs to live.
  • Tissues are groups of cells with a similar structure and function, for example muscles are tissues made up of muscular cells.
  • Organs are groups of tissues working together, such as the heart.
  • Organ systems are groups of organs which have a very specific function and all rely on each other, such as the circulatory system.
  • An organism - like you! - is made up of several organ systems.
