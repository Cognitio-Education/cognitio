Cell Structure

Revision Notes

Cells are the basic building blocks in all living things. They perform important functions in the body.

There are two types of cell we look at at GCSE:

  • Animal cells

  • Plant cells

Cells have a structure, in that they have specific things inside them in a certain arrangement. These things are called organelles.

Animal Cells

(Image by domdomegg - Own work, CC BY 4.0)

Animal cells contain the following things:

  • Cytoplasm: A gel in which the cell's chemical reactions take place. All of the organelles are suspended within the cytoplasm.

  • Nucleus: Controls everything that the cell does by containing the DNA (sets of biological instructions) for the cell.

  • Cell Membrane: Controls what goes into and out of the cell.

  • Mitochondria: The "powerhouses" of the cell. They perform aerobic respiration in order to provide energy to the cell.

Not on the diagram is:

  • Ribosomes: Synthesises (makes) proteins for the cell.

Plant Cells

(Image by domdomegg - Own work, CC BY 4.0)

Plant cells contain everything that animal cells contain, but with the following additional things:

  • Cell Wall: This is made of cellulose, a sugar. Provides support and structure to the cell.

  • Chloroplasts: Only found in cells in green parts of a plant. They contain chlorophyll (which makes the cells and plant green) and perfrom photosynthesis in order to obtain food for the plant.

  • Vacuole: A space filled with cell sap. Keeps the cell rigid.
